    Words are ghosted bowmen, the haunters of the heart, 
    conscripts of the learning tower, its stepped degrees 
    twisted, bruised by failed tides of forbidden fruit. 
    Their tendrils constrict all life to functional form, 
    diluting echoes of meaning, strands of decay. 
    Apexed eye skips its lancing quest across the greyed, 
    perchance unveiling position or ambition,  
    the enlightened ignorance of its foundation. 
    The Rose remains pierced, beaten, besting the glancing 
    blows until the bowmen and arrows become sticks, 
    the confused tower falls and all that will remain 

    slow poling cadence, drawing souls into the shade.
